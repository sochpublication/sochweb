Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a Publication?

A publication is the process of producing and distributing books, articles, or other written content to the public. At Soch, we help authors publish their work with professionalism and visibility.

What is a Solo Book?

A solo book is a publication authored by a single writer. The author has full control over the content, theme, and message, making it a personal and powerful form of expression.

What is an Anthology?

An anthology is a collection of writings—such as poems, stories, or essays—by multiple writers, usually centered around a common theme. Each writer contributes a part of the book.

Who is a Compiler?

A compiler is the person who gathers, organizes, and edits content from multiple writers for an anthology. They ensure coherence, formatting, and quality across all submissions.

What is the Role of a Project Head?

A project head acts as a compiler and oversees the project from start to finish, ensuring smooth coordination and successful completion.

How to Participate in an Anthology?

Visit our website to find current anthology projects. Show your interest by filling out the provided form. Once selected, you will receive guidelines and deadlines for your submission.

I Want to Compile a Book, What is the Process?

Contact us on social media: Instagram or Facebook. We will guide you through the process of compiling a book, including theme selection, writer recruitment, and project management.
